GroceryGuide

Groceries for the blind and visually impaired

Our project consists of a grocery assistant system for the visually impaired. This technology would include at least two modes (subject to the serviced group's interests), including one where a user loads or inputs a grocery list tagged by product category and they would get haptic feedback in the store whenever in proximity to any of the yet-alerted items (i.e. in physical proximity to the product category in the store), and an exploratory mode without any pre-populated shopping list where such sensors in the store would give a verbal notification to the user of which food category they are passing whenever they come into proximity of one such sensor. Our hopes are that this system will facilitate mobility and improve the shopping experience for visually impaired people.

About Us

We are a group of mostly undergrads, and one PHD student, with a wide variety of backgrounds: computational linguistics, digital humanities, backend coding, frontend coding, database architecture, natural language processing, and more. We all share a love of coding and a desire to help disabled people live more fulfilling lives!
